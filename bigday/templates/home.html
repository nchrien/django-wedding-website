About Albuquerque

We are excited to invite our family and friends to celebrate with us in the Land of Enchantment! At 5,312 feet above sea level, Albuquerque is a high desert environment. If you haven't spent much time at high altitude before, be sure to wear sunscreen and stay hydrated. In addition, be aware that alcohol may affect you more than it does at sea level.

Getting Here

The Albuquerque International Sunport is a 15 minute drive from Old Town and 20 minutes from Los Poblanos. Just take I-25 North to I-40 West, then exit onto Rio Grande Boulevard (South for Old Town, North for Los Ranchos). Old Town and Los Ranchos hotels do not typically offer complimentary airport shuttles, but there is a paid shuttle service available from the airport if you choose not to rent a car. In addition, ride sharing services (Uber and Lyft) are cost effective and easily available in Albuquerque.

Things to Do

Albuquerque in general and the Old Town neighborhood in particular are home to a variety of activities and attractions. Some of the highlights include:

Museums

Old Town is Albuquerque's primary museum district. The New Mexico Museum of Natural History, Explora science center, and the Albuquerque Museum of Art and History are all within two blocks of Hotel Albuquerque. Just a mile down the road is the ABQ Biopark, which includes a zoo, aquarium, and botanic garden (pictured below). Other museum highlights in the city include the Albuquerque International Balloon Museum, the National Museum of Nuclear Science & History, the Indian Pueblo Cultural Center, and the National Hispanic Cultural Center.
