About Albuquerque

We are excited to invite our friends and family to celebrate with us in New Mexico, the Land of Enchantment! At 5,312 feet above sea level, Albuquerque is a high desert environment. If you haven't spent much time at high altitude before, be sure to wear sunscreen and drink plenty of water. In addition, be aware that alcohol may affect you more than it does at sea level.

Getting Here

The Albuquerque International Sunport is a 15 minute drive from Old Town and 20 minutes from Los Poblanos. Just take I-25 North to I-40 West, then exit onto Rio Grande Boulevard (South for Old Town, North for Los Ranchos). Old Town and Los Ranchos hotels do not generally offer complimentary airport shuttles, but there is a paid shuttle service available from the airport if you choose not to rent a car. In addition, ride sharing services (Uber and Lyft) are cost effective and easily available in Albuquerque.

Where to Stay

There are many good hotels in Albuquerque, but we recommend the ones listed below in Old Town, all of which are 10 minutes down the road from Los Poblanos. Remember that this is Labor Day weekend, so it's a good idea to book your accomodations early.


Hotel Albuquerque at Old Town - A landmark of Old Town, the historic Hotel Albuquerque will be hosting most of the weekend's social events. We have arranged a courtesy block of guest rooms there under the code [CODE].

Hotel Chaco - Immediately next door to Hotel Albuquerque and newly opened in 2017, Hotel Chaco offers a tranquil luxury experience.

Best Western Plus Rio Grande Inn - Just two blocks up the street, this Old Town mainstay offers quality accomodations at a great value.


There is an inn at Los Poblanos, but be aware that it has a limited number of rooms which tend to book several months in advance during peak periods such as Labor Day weekend.
