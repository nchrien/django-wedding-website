About Albuquerque

We are excited to invite our family and friends to celebrate with us in the Land of Enchantment! At 5,312 feet above sea level, Albuquerque is a high desert environment. If you haven't spent much time at high altitude before, be sure to wear sunscreen and stay hydrated. In addition, be aware that alcohol may affect you more than it does at sea level.

Getting Here

The Albuquerque International Sunport is a 15 minute drive from Old Town and 20 minutes from Los Poblanos. Just take I-25 North to I-40 West, then exit onto Rio Grande Boulevard (South for Old Town, North for Los Ranchos). Old Town and Los Ranchos hotels do not typically offer complimentary airport shuttles, but there is a paid shuttle service available from the airport if you choose not to rent a car. In addition, ride sharing services (Uber and Lyft) are cost effective and easily available in Albuquerque.

Things to Do

Albuquerque in general and the Old Town neighborhood in particular are home to a variety of activities and attractions. Some of the highlights include:

Museums

Old Town is Albuquerque's primary museum district. The New Mexico Museum of Natural History, Explora science center, and the Albuquerque Museum of Art and History are all within two blocks of Hotel Albuquerque. Just a mile down the road is the ABQ Biopark, which includes a zoo, aquarium, and botanic garden (pictured below). Other museum highlights in the city include the Albuquerque International Balloon Museum, the National Museum of Nuclear Science & History, the University Art Museum, the Indian Pueblo Cultural Center, and the National Hispanic Cultural Center.

Arts and Culture

Albuquerque has a vibrant cultural scene. In addition to its museums, the city is home to over a hundred galleries and studios, mostly concentrated in the Old Town, Downtown, and Nob Hill neighborhoods. In the performing arts realm, Albuquerque is home to the National Institute of Flamenco, which has regular perfomances (including tapas dinner shows every Thursday through Sunday at Tablao Flamenco in Hotel Albuquerque). There are regular theatrical and musical performances at Popejoy Hall, the Albuquerque Little Theater, and the KiMo Theater. The casinos just outside of town regularly feature major touring shows.

Outdoor Recreation

New Mexico is known for its natural beauty, and Albuquerque is no exception. There are hundreds of miles of hiking trails within and just outside the city, including the Paseo Del Bosque trail through the Rio Grande Valley State Park, just West of Old Town and Los Ranchos, and even more scenic areas such as the Sandia Foothills. Arguably the jewel of Albuquerque's outdoor spaces is Petroglyph National Monument, featuring Native American rock carvings as much as 700 years old. Another top activity is the Sandia Peak Tramway, a 2.7 mile cable car ride up to the 10,378 foot Sandia Peak, featuring spectacular views of the Sandias and the Rio Grande Valley.

Cuisine

New Mexican cuisine is a unique fusion of Spanish, Mexican, and Native American ingredients and techniques. Red and green chile are a signature ingredient in many dishes. There are many excellent restaurants, New Mexican and otherwise, in Albuquerque; some highlights in the Old Town and Downtown areas include Church Street Café, Cecilia's Café, Monroe's, The Cellar, Seasons Rotisserie & Grill, St. Clair Winery & Bistro, High Noon Restaurant & Saloon, and Le Troquet. Los Poblanos' restaurant, Campo, is also highly acclaimed.
